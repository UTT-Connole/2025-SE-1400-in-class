The Season of Change

Autumn is nature's grand finale before winter's quiet slumber. As temperatures drop and daylight shortens, trees prepare for their seasonal rest by shedding their leaves in a spectacular display of color.

Why Leaves Fall

As chlorophyll breaks down, the green color fades and reveals the vibrant reds, oranges, and yellows that were hidden all summer. Trees form a protective layer at the base of each leaf stem, eventually causing the leaves to detach and gently fall to the ground.

Autumn Colors

Each species of tree displays its own unique palette of autumn colors, creating a magnificent tapestry across the landscape.

The Poetry of Falling

There's something deeply peaceful about watching leaves drift down on a crisp autumn day. They dance and swirl on gentle breezes, carpeting the ground in a rustling blanket of gold and crimson that crunches satisfyingly underfoot.
